Cookies

This cookies notice was last updated on 24 May 2018.

To help improve this site as well as enable access to restricted content we place small files, known as cookies, onto your computer.

We use cookies to:

  • enable you to login to access subscription-only content
  • remember if you’ve completed a user satisfaction survey, or requested not to be asked to complete it, so you don’t get asked again
  • measure how you use the website so we can provide usage statistics as well as improve it to meet your needs
  • enable us to embed YouTube videos on the website showing you how to use the service

You can manage these small files as you wish. You can even decide to stop any site from using them.

More information can be found at aboutcookies.org or allaboutcookies.org. You can also read the Jisc web team's blog on cookie law.

How we use cookies

We use cookies for several purposes on this website. Some are essential to enable the platform to function, others are not essential but help to improve the user experience, enable us to provide usage statistics, help us to understand how the platform is used and provide the ability to embed videos on our support pages showing you how to use the service.

We’ve listed each of them below with more details about why we use them and how long they’ll last.

Essential cookies

Cookies for authentication

These cookies are required to enable the platform to function, to enable you to log in to access subscription-only content. If you do not accept these cookies then you will not be able to log in to the platform via the UK Access Management Federation (Shibboleth and OpenAthens).

Name Purpose Expires
idp_session If you login to the service via the UK Access Management Federation (Shibboleth and OpenAthens) then a session cookie is stored on your machine. Session cookies are cleared when you close your browser. Cookies are part of the user identification process and without these you will not be able to authenticate via the UK Access Management Federation and use the service. 1 hour

Non-essential cookies

Measuring website usage (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics to collect information about how people use our site to make sure it’s meeting your needs and to understand how we could improve it.

We also use Google Analytics to collect usage statistics to enable us to create reports showing how the service and collections within it are used. Usage statistics reports are provided to subscribing institutions and used by Jisc.

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking.

Google Analytics stores information about:

  • the pages you visit
  • how long you spend on each page
  • how you got to the site
  • what you click on while you're here

We do not collect or store your personal information (eg your name or address) so this information cannot be used to identify who you are.

The following cookies are set by Google Analytics:

Name Purpose Expires
_utma This helps us count how many people visit the site by tracking if you’ve visited before, so we can count how many of our visitors are new to the site or to a certain page. 2 years
_utmb This works with _utmc to calculate the average length of time you spend on the site. 30 minutes
_utmc This works with _utmb to calculate when you close your browser. when you close your browser
_utmt This is used by Google Analytics to ensure its work doesn’t have a big impact on the site’s performance.
_utmz This tells us how you reached the site (like from another website or a search engine). 6 months

Cookies for survey

User satisfaction surveys are usually only on the website for around a month once a year.

Name Purpose Expires
COOKIE_NAME_TBC To record whether you have completed the user satisfaction survey. This is to ensure you do not keep getting the survey prompt each time you go to our home page. If you complete the survey the cookie expires at the end of the survey period. If you select 'not today' the cookie expires 24 hours later.

Name Purpose Expires
VISITOR_INFO1_LIVE This tries to estimate your bandwidth and deliver a video of appropriate quality. 240 days
YSC A unique identify given to a user on a site with videos. when you close your browser
GEUP Allows Google to track viewers across sites that use YouTube videos. 2 years
PREF Allows Google to track viewers across sites that use YouTube videos. 240 days
