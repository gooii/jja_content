Privacy and Cookies

What are cookies and what are they used for?

Cookies are small text files that are stored by your web browser.
They are primarily used to remember if users have visited the website before, and to improve the way that sites work.
We use them for authenticating users and to record anonymised usage information.

More information can be found at aboutcookies.org or allaboutcookies.org

Authentication

The Shibboleth cookie for authenticated user sessions is called idp_session. These expire after an hour.

You can read more about Shibboleth session cookies here: https://wiki.shibboleth.net/confluence/display/SHIB2/IdPCookieUsage

Measuring Website Usage

We use Google Analytics to measure usage of the service.
The cookies generated by Google Analytics are only available to Journal Archives and do not reveal any information that would cause users to be personally identifiable.
More information on Google Analytics cookie usage can be found here: https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/analyticsjs/cookie-usage

Surveys

Periodically we will add a popup survey to the Journal Archives homepage to gather feedback from users.
This survey is not active for the majority of the year but is usually in effect for the month of November.
The cookie generated by this survey is called COOKIE_NAME_TBC

Managing and deleting cookies

We use cookies to improve the way our website works, and we don't gather any personally identifiable information.
Blocking or deleting the cookies will impact on the way that the site works and you could lose some functionality.
However, you can control and manage cookies if you wish.
