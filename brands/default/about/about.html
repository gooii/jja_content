Journal Archives: one platform, 8 publisher archives, over 600 journals

Journal Archives is a Jisc service containing the backfiles from 8 major publishers: Brill, Institution of Civil Engineers, Institute of Physics, ProQuest, Oxford University Press, Cambridge University Press, the Royal Society of Chemistry and Taylor and Francis as well as the complete run of the feminist magazine Spare Rib.

Covering a wide range of subject areas including science, social science, humanities, law, energy and environment and incorporating over 600 journals, it enables students and researchers to:

  • Trace the origins and development of major theories and discoveries
  • Find out how your field of study emerged and evolved
  • Explore the earliest articles of famous authors
  • Review the literature on which your latest studies are founded
  • Understand the themes and preoccupations of previous research generations

Spare Rib magazine is freely available. The rest of Journal Archives can be searched, but access to view and download articles is only available via subscription to UK HE and FE Institutions and Research Councils who are full members of Jisc Collections.

Key features

  • Cross-search the backfiles of 8 publisher archives in a single platform
  • View and search the full text as well as the bibliographic information (e.g. author, article title, journal title, ISSN) for all articles
  • Download articles, as well as whole issues of Spare Rib magazine, as PDFs

Benefits to researchers

  • Time saving, cross-search 8 publisher archives and download articles instantly using a single, simple to use interface
  • The only place online to browse whole issues, and search individual articles, from the iconic feminist magazine Spare Rib

Benefits to institutions

  • Journal Archives is included in the Jisc VAT cost sharing group, so for members of the group there is no VAT on the subscription fee
  • A single, simple to use platform to support users on for eight publisher archives

Background

Find out more about why the Jisc Historic Books, Jisc Journal Archives and Jisc MediaHub services were set up originally and some of the challenges we encountered along the way in this Insights article by Caren Milloy, Head of Projects, Jisc Collections: http://insights.uksg.org/articles/10.1629/2048-7754.25.1.74/

  • Jisc Historic Books was replaced by the Historical Texts platform in June 2014.
  • Jisc Journal Archives was replaced by the Journal Archives platform (https://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk/) in January 2015.
  • Jisc MediaHub was decommissioned in September 2016 (https://www.jisc.ac.uk/mediahub) and replaced by MediaPlus provided by Alexander Street (http://alexanderstreet.com/products/mediaplus).

Subscription options

Subscription options vary for HE and FE institutions and a free 30 day trial is available to eligible institutions. The subscription fee is pro-rata, so if you subscribe part way through the subscription year you only pay for the remaining months for that year rather than the whole year.

For any enquiries about subscription options please contact the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk.

UK Higher Education (HE) and Research Councils

Subscription Options for 2017-2020:

This subscription package runs from 1st August 2017 through 31st July 2020.

  • Jisc eCollections: Historical Texts and Journal Archives for HE 2017-2020 (includes Journal Archives and Historical Texts)

Subscription Options for 2014-2017:

These subscription packages run from 1st August 2014 through 31st July 2017.

  • Jisc eCollections for Higher Education Institutions 2014-2017 (includes Journal Archives, Historical Texts and MediaPlus)
  • Jisc eCollections: Historical Texts and Journal Archives for HE 2014-2017 (includes Historical Texts and Journal Archives but not MediaPlus)

It is not possible to subscribe to Journal Archives on its own.

UK Further Education (FE)

The following subscription package is available:

Jisc eCollections: Journal Archives and Historical Texts for FE 2014-2017

This provides access to both Historical Texts and Journal Archives. It is not possible to subscribe to Journal Archives on its own.

Free 30 day trial

UK HE and FE institutions and Research Councils who are full members of Jisc Collections and do not currently subscribe to Journal Archives can request a free 30 day trial.

A librarian at the institution can do this by contacting the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk or logging in to their account on the Jisc Collections website.
