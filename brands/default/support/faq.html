Can I access Journal Archives?

Journal Archives is available via subscription to UK Higher Education and Further Education institutions and Research Councils who are full members of Jisc Collections. Eligible institutions can request a free 30 day trial of the resource. For any subscription enquiries please contact the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk.

The complete run of the Feminist magazine “Spare Rib” is open-access and free for all to use. It can be searched via the Spare Rib landing page found at: https://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk/britishlibrary/sparerib, as well as in via the main Journal Archives site.

Troubleshooting tips

As a first step, if you experience any problems using Journal Archives we would suggest you clear your browser cache and cookies then restart your browser. The Journal Archives application will save information in your browser, so if you've used the service previously, then clearing this information ensures you're seeing the latest version of the application. We would recommend clearing your browser cache and cookies after we release updates to the service for this reason.

Information on how to clear your cache and cookies in various browsers:

  • Internet Explorer 11
  • Firefox
  • Chrome
  • Safari

The preferred route to access Journal Archives is as follows:

  • Browse to the following link: https://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk/home
  • Click the Login button on the Navigation Bar (it looks like an arrow facing to the right)
  • Click the Login button on the popup window that appears
  • At the next page, search for your institution by name, select it from the list and click the “Continue” button
  • When you arrive at your institutional login page, enter your credentials

You should now be returned to the Journal Archives homepage, successfully authenticated.

If you're still experiencing difficulties, please contact the Journal Archives helpdesk at journalarchives@jisc.ac.uk. Please include your institutional affiliation, which computer operating system you are using (e.g.Windows 7, Mac OSX etc), which web browsing software you are using (e.g.Chrome, Safari, Internet Explorer) and details of the route you are using to access the service (e.g. a link from your library service, a wayfless URL etc) and we'll be in touch to assist.

Do you provide WAYFless URLs?

Yes, we provide WAYFless URLs for Journal Archives.

You can use the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool to generate one for your institition.

  • Go to the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool
  • Select your institution as the "Identity Provider"
  • Select "Journal Archives" as the Service Provider
  • Accept the default target URL of "https://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk/ "

A WAYFless URL will be displayed for your institution for you to copy or email to yourself.

What format are the articles in?

All articles are available in PDF format. You can download Adobe Reader free from the Adobe website if you do not have a PDF reader on your machine.

Can I download the articles?

Yes, all articles can be downloaded as PDFs. You can download Adobe Reader free from the Adobe website if you do not have a PDF reader on your machine.

Why have I been signed out of Journal Archives unexpectedly?

Journal Archives will sign out inactive sessions after an hour, approximately. There are other factors that can also cause you to be signed out unexpectedly that are outside of our domain, including your institutional provider and the route they use to authenticate you, so this cannot be guaranteed as a fixed amount of time. Users are best advised to download or make a note of any articles or searches of interest before leaving their computer for any extended period.
