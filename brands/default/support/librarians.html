Information for Librarians

This page is designed to provide key information to help librarians at subscribing institutions set up and manage access to Journal Archives. If you have a question that is not answered below please contact our helpdesk at journalarchives@jisc.ac.uk and we will be happy to assist you.

Subscriptions

Journal Archives is available via subscription to UK Higher Education and Further Education institutions and Research Councils who are full members of Jisc Collections. Eligible institutions can request a free 30 day trial of the resource. For any subscription enquiries please contact the Jisc Collections helpdesk at: help.digitalresources@jisc.ac.uk.

Authentication

We provide access via the UK Access Management Federation (Shibboleth/OpenAthens) as well as IP authentication. You can have one or both of these methods enabled. If you want to check the authentication options enabled for your institition for Journal Archives, or make any changes to them, please contact our helpdesk at journalarchives@jisc.ac.uk

WAYFless URLs

We provide WAYFless URLs for Journal Archives.

You can use the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool to generate one for your institition.

  • Go to the WAYFless URL generator (WUGEN) tool
  • Select your institution as the "Identity Provider"
  • Select "Journal Archives" as the Service Provider
  • Accept the default target URL of "https://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk/ "

A WAYFless URL will be displayed for your institution for you to copy or email to yourself.

Proxy servers

If you access Journal Archives using IP authentication, and you have a proxy server at your institution which you wish to be able to use for accessing the resource, please let our helpdesk know your proxy serversâ€™ IP address, if it is not included in the range you have provided for your institution.

You may also need to add the following entries to your proxy server forward table:

  • http://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk
  • https://journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk
  • https://data.journalarchives.jisc.ac.uk

If you use EZProxy then you can use the EZProxy stanza for Journal Archives which is available on the OCLC support pages.

Adding Journal Archives to your Athens resource list

Journal Archives is available via UK Access Management Federation authentication (Shibboleth and OpenAthens) and it is not an Athens protected resource. To add it to your Athens resource list it will need to be added as an "additional resource" by your institutions Athens Administrator.

If you would like instructions on how to do this please contact the OpenAthens service desk.

KBART Files

We are currently preparing new Knowledge Base And Related Tools (KBART) files to reflect the current contents of the Journal Archives service. They are still in development, and we hope to be able to make them available to institutions soon. The new KBART files will be in the KBART 2 format.

More information on KBART and the KBART 2 specification can be found at the NISO website.

Usage statistics

A new usage statistics portal service for Journal Archives is currently in development. In the meantime, we have been logging usage statistics for Journal Archives from 15 January 2015 (the date access to Jisc Journal Archives was be switched off). Further details on how to access these statistics will be made available once the portal service is brought online. We are currently testing the portal and should hopefully be able to make it available soon.

You will still be able to access historical statistics for Jisc Journal Archives from the old Jisc Journal Archives usage statistics website for now. We will ensure that access to archived Jisc Journal Archives statistics will be possible when the statistics website for Journal Archives is set up.

Maintenance

We schedule our routine maintenance work on Tuesday mornings between 07:00 and 09:00, where possible.

We provide 10 working days notice of any planned maintenance by emailing the Jisc eCollections JiscMail list to inform librarians at subscribing institutions, the Journal Archives JiscMail list , which is open to anyone, and putting a message in an orange alert box on our homepage as well as on Twitter.

Keeping up to date

All primary contacts at subscribing institutions are automatically added to the Jisc eCollections mailing list, where information for all three services in the Jisc eCollections service (Historical Texts, Journal Archives and MediaHub) is posted. Additionally, there is a Journal Archives JiscMail list which is open to anyone who would like to keep up to date with the latest news, developments and events for the Journal Archives service. We also have Twitter and Facebook accounts.
